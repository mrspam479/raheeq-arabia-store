# 03 — ICP & Positioning

## 1. ICP one-liner

**Saudi-Arabian woman, 22–42, urban (Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, Khobar, Mecca, Medina), middle-to-upper-middle income, beauty-aware, social-media-native, COD-trusting.** She buys with her heart and validates with proof. She wants to look and feel her best **without** being sold to like a tourist.

## 2. Three persona snapshots

### Persona A — Sara, 26, Riyadh, recently engaged

- Discovers us on Snapchat or TikTok.
- Spends 90 min/day on social, buys 1–2 beauty products a week.
- Pain: hair shedding from stress + heat-styling, can't find a "premium but Saudi" brand she's proud to be seen with.
- Desire: feel ready for engagement photos, dewy skin, glossy hair.
- Buys: **حبّة جذر + حبّة نورة**. AOV target: 349–500 SAR.

### Persona B — Hessa, 33, Jeddah, working mom of two

- Lives on Instagram + TikTok between school runs.
- Sleeps badly, drains coffee, feels her skin "thirsty".
- Pain: exhausted, dull skin, "أنا تعبت" — guilty for taking care of herself.
- Desire: a *ritual* that feels indulgent but is functional.
- Buys: **حبّة ليالي + حبّة نورة**. AOV target: 349 SAR. High return purchaser if delivery is on time.

### Persona C — Nora, 39, Khobar, executive

- Disposable income, picky.
- Researches ingredients before buying.
- Pain: hair thinning around the part-line, fine lines, knows the science.
- Desire: clinical proof, ingredient transparency, premium packaging.
- Buys: **حبّة جذر (3-pack)**. AOV target: 349 SAR. Becomes the brand's word-of-mouth driver.

## 3. Jobs to be done

When the ICP buys Raheeq, she hires us to:

1. **Feel cared for.** A small daily ritual that says "you matter".
2. **Look visibly better** — hair, skin, sleep — in 30–60 days.
3. **Display taste.** The box looks beautiful on her vanity (and her Instagram story).
4. **Trust the source.** Saudi-owned, lab-tested, transparent ingredients.
5. **Order without friction.** No card. No English checkout. No callback in broken Arabic.

## 6. Positioning statement (used as our hero promise)

> For Saudi women who want a daily ritual that actually works,
> **رحيق** is the Arab-made, lab-tested gummy line that makes hair, skin, and sleep care feel like a treat —
> backed by clinical-grade ingredients and Khaleeji craftsmanship,
> shipped to your door with cash-on-delivery.

## 7. Where she comes from (acquisition context)

- **Snapchat Discover ads** (50% of media spend) — vertical UGC of a Khaleeji woman talking to the camera, ASMR product reveal, "وش جربتي حبّة جذر؟". Lands on PDP.
- **TikTok Spark Ads** (35%) — creator UGC, "خلّوني أوريكم سرّي الجديد".
- **Meta IG Reels** (15%) — editorial product mood + ingredient close-ups, link in bio to PDP.

Therefore:

- **Mobile is king.** Build mobile-first. Desktop is a thank-you, not a hero.
- **First fold must mirror the ad's promise** — same hero benefit + same product visual.
- **No new tabs.** No surprise pop-ups. The funnel is one tab, four interactions: scroll → choose offer → submit form → upsell.

## 8. Anti-ICP (do not optimize for)

- Customers who pay by card and want a Western checkout flow.
- Customers shopping for the lowest price (we are not the discount brand).
- Customers outside KSA. (We can serve them later; the site does not promise it now.)

## 9. Cultural & religious considerations

- All visuals respect modesty norms. Models photographed waist-up, no skin emphasis beyond face and hands.
- Avoid scheduling ad pushes during prayer times in copy ("بعد العشاء" is fine; "before Maghrib" is not used in product timing).
- Ramadan-friendly framing: gummies are non-pork, non-bovine, **plant-based gelatin (pectin)**.
- Use lunar references gently — "ليلة هادية" works; do **not** invoke religious imagery for sales.

## 10. Founder voice cue (used in About + small story blocks)

Sign-off lines on long pages use first-person plural ("نحن نختار…") — never first-person singular except inside the About narrative.
